About

Japhet Khendlo is a distinguished Geospatial Specialist and Lecturer at Mzuzu University in Malawi, with deep expertise in geospatial technology, climate change, and environmental management. Holding a Master’s Degree in Geoinformatics and a Bachelor’s Degree in Land Surveying, Japhet is currently pursuing a PhD in the Application of Geospatial Analysis in Climate Change and Environmental Modelling at the University of Mascareignes, Mauritius.

Japhet's research focuses on enhancing community resilience against hydrometeorological hazards through the use of advanced geospatial technologies and AI. His ongoing Doctoral Thesis delves into integrating geospatial technologies and AI to address gaps in risk assessment and disaster preparedness. Past work includes impactful studies on traffic accidents, sewage treatment site suitability, and surface runoff potential, showcasing his broad application of GIS and Remote Sensing in environmental and infrastructural contexts.

In addition to his academic and research endeavors, Japhet is actively involved in mentoring and coaching students and professionals, providing guidance for career development and academic pursuits. He engages in community service and advocacy, working to ensure safety from natural disasters and fighting against sex slavery and child labour. Japhet has also launched a student-led Youth Mappers organization at Mzuzu University, fostering learning about open street mapping and contributing to humanitarian efforts.

Japhet excels in teaching and course development, creating educational content and supervising student projects. His technical skills encompass geospatial analysis, engineering surveying, and data analysis using AI tools. He is known for his impactful presentation skills at conferences and employs creative problem-solving methods for analyzing geospatial and environmental data. Additionally, he provides specialized training in GIS and remote sensing for groundwater professionals and actively contributes to research dissemination.

Japhet's commitment to integrating geospatial insights with practical applications underscores his dedication to advancing sustainable development and resilience in communities, both locally and across the SADC region.
